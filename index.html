Who We Are and What We Do

Founded by students, NAHS Debate is a group of students eager to improve their communication, logic, and public speaking skills in a competitive and fun environment.

  • Participate in the most professional and competitive debate tournaments across the country.
  • Prepare our debaters to perform well through the use of effective logic, reasoning, and rhetoric.
  • Establish effective public speaking methods to make our debaters effective communicators.
  • Use research to understand both sides of a debate and present ourselves as polished logicians.
  • Research and learn about current and past events to gain a richer understanding of the world.

Our mission: The NAHS Debate Team aims to improve the critical thinking, research, and communication skills of our students through interscholastic competition in competitive debate.

What is Competitive Debate?

Competitive debate is an official form of interscholastic high school debate which is run by the National Speech and Debate Association (NSDA). Competitive debates are generally more structured and more professional than normal, everyday debates. Debaters often have to argue both sides of a topic and have to use significant research, facts, and statistics to back up their arguments. In addition, tournaments run like a sports season, with a new topic every month and tournaments using brackets, seeding to add to the competitiveness of the debates.

What are the benefits of debate?

Research has shown debate to be a beneficial and meaningful extracurricular in general. Benefits of debate include increased awareness and appreciation for both sides of political issues, increased communication and public speaking skills, improvement of critical thinking and research skills, improvement of personal leadership qualities and interpersonal relationships, and more. Debate is useful for whatever field you decide to go into, as it improves research and writing skills as well. More information can be found in "Why Debate?".



How much of a time commitment is debate?

Debate is an extracurricular which can take as little or as much of your time as you want it to. You can attend whichever tournaments you want you; you can go to one tournament, you can go to all of them, or somewhere in between. The amount of preparation you put forth for debate is up to you as well, though teams who prepare more are generally more successful. Debate is also compatible with sports. We meet in the period of time between the end of the school day and the start of sports practices. Meetings are not mandatory, though are reccomended. There is usually only 1 or 2 tournaments each month (usually on a Sunday or a Saturday), making debate compatible with sports matches/meets as well.

Why should I join debate?

As we have stated above, debate has numerous benefits regardless of what your profession will be in the future. The skills you learn are those you will carry throughout your life. Most of all, we are a fun and supportive team, and debate is not just a competition: it's a social group, and you will make friendships, bonds, and connections which will last a lifetime.

Still not convinced? Check out our "Why Debate?" section! Competitive debate is one of America's premiere extracurriculars which has been proven to improve on life skills, leadership, critical thinking, research, and more. It also looks good on college apps!
